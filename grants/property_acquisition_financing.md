# Property Acquisition Financing Guide
## 2072 E Roxboro Rd NE, Brookhaven, GA 30324

This guide outlines financing strategies for acquiring the multifamily property at 2072 E Roxboro Rd NE, focusing on leveraging VA loan approval while exploring additional funding sources for closing costs and alternative financing options.

## VA Loan Primary Strategy

### VA Loan Overview
- **Approval Amount**: Up to $650,000
- **Key Benefits**:
  - No down payment required
  - No private mortgage insurance (PMI)
  - Competitive interest rates
  - Flexible credit requirements
  - Limited closing costs

### VA Loan for Multifamily Properties
- **Property Requirements**:
  - Must be 2-4 units
  - Veteran must occupy one of the units as primary residence
  - Property must meet VA minimum property requirements (MPRs)
  
- **Loan Limits**:
  - DeKalb County VA loan limit: $726,200 (2023)
  - For multi-unit properties, limits may be higher
  - With full entitlement, no loan limit applies

### Maximizing VA Loan Benefits
1. **Occupancy Strategy**:
   - Plan to occupy one unit for minimum 12 months
   - After occupancy requirement met, can convert to full rental property
   
2. **Rental Income Consideration**:
   - VA allows counting anticipated rental income from other units
   - Typically 75% of rental income can be used to qualify
   - Need market rent analysis from appraiser
   
3. **VA Funding Fee Reduction/Exemption**:
   - Reduced funding fee for subsequent VA loan use
   - Possible exemption with VA disability rating
   - Can be rolled into loan amount

## Supplemental Financing for Closing Costs

### Federal Programs

#### 1. HUD Good Neighbor Next Door Program
- **Overview**: Offers 50% discount on HUD homes for veterans and other eligible professionals
- **Requirements**: Property must be in revitalization area; 36-month occupancy
- **Application**: Through HUD-registered real estate agent
- **Website**: https://www.hud.gov/program_offices/housing/sfh/reo/goodn/gnndabot

#### 2. Homes for Heroes
- **Overview**: Provides rebates and discounts on home purchases for veterans
- **Benefit**: Average of $2,400 in savings through affiliated agents and lenders
- **Application**: Register on their website for local specialist
- **Website**: https://www.homesforheroes.com/

### State and Local Programs

#### 1. Georgia Dream Homeownership Program
- **Agency**: Georgia Department of Community Affairs
- **Overview**: Down payment assistance for first-time homebuyers and veterans
- **Benefit**: Up to $7,500 in assistance (potentially more in certain areas)
- **Requirements**: Income limits apply; homebuyer education course required
- **Application**: Through participating lenders
- **Website**: https://www.dca.ga.gov/safe-affordable-housing/homeownership/georgia-dream

#### 2. DeKalb County First-Time Homebuyer Program
- **Agency**: DeKalb County Community Development Department
- **Overview**: Down payment and closing cost assistance
- **Benefit**: Up to $10,000 in assistance
- **Requirements**: Income limits apply; property must be in DeKalb County
- **Application**: Through DeKalb County Community Development
- **Website**: https://www.dekalbcountyga.gov/community-development/first-time-homebuyer-program

#### 3. Invest Atlanta Downpayment Assistance
- **Agency**: Invest Atlanta
- **Overview**: Down payment assistance for properties in Atlanta
- **Benefit**: Up to $20,000 in assistance
- **Requirements**: Income limits apply; homebuyer education required
- **Application**: Through participating lenders
- **Website**: https://www.investatlanta.com/homebuyers/homebuyer-incentives

### Nonprofit and Private Programs

#### 1. Veterans Association of Real Estate Professionals (VAREP)
- **Overview**: Down payment assistance grants for veterans
- **Benefit**: Varies by program and location
- **Application**: Through VAREP chapters
- **Website**: https://varep.net/

#### 2. PenFed Foundation Dream Makers Program
- **Overview**: Matching grant program for veterans and military members
- **Benefit**: Grants up to $5,000 for down payment and closing costs
- **Requirements**: First-time homebuyer; income requirements
- **Application**: Through PenFed Foundation
- **Website**: https://penfedfoundation.org/apply-for-assistance/dream-makers/

#### 3. Operation Homefront Homes on the Homefront
- **Overview**: Program that provides homes to eligible veterans
- **Benefit**: Potential mortgage-free homeownership
- **Requirements**: Specific eligibility criteria for veterans
- **Application**: Through Operation Homefront
- **Website**: https://www.operationhomefront.org/homes-on-the-homefront/

## Alternative Financing Strategies

### 1. FHA 203(k) Rehabilitation Loan
- **Overview**: Finances both purchase and rehabilitation in one loan
- **Benefit**: Low down payment (3.5%); can finance repairs/improvements
- **Requirements**: Property must meet eligibility requirements
- **Loan Limits**: $472,030 for single-family; higher for multi-unit properties
- **Application**: Through FHA-approved lenders
- **Website**: https://www.hud.gov/program_offices/housing/sfh/203k

### 2. Fannie Mae HomeStyle Renovation Loan
- **Overview**: Conventional loan that includes renovation financing
- **Benefit**: One loan for purchase and renovation; potentially lower MI than FHA
- **Down Payment**: As low as 3% for primary residence
- **Application**: Through participating lenders
- **Website**: https://singlefamily.fanniemae.com/originating-underwriting/mortgage-products/homestyle-renovation

### 3. Freddie Mac CHOICERenovation Loan
- **Overview**: Similar to HomeStyle; finances purchase and renovation
- **Benefit**: One loan closing; flexible renovation options
- **Down Payment**: As low as 3% for primary residence
- **Application**: Through participating lenders
- **Website**: https://sf.freddiemac.com/working-with-us/origination-underwriting/mortgage-products/choicerenovation

### 4. USDA Rural Development Loan
- **Overview**: No down payment loans for rural properties
- **Benefit**: 100% financing; competitive rates
- **Requirements**: Income limits; property must be in eligible rural area
- **Application**: Through USDA-approved lenders
- **Website**: https://www.rd.usda.gov/programs-services/single-family-housing-programs

### 5. Owner Financing with Seller
- **Strategy**: Negotiate directly with seller for partial or full financing
- **Potential Benefits**: 
  - Lower closing costs
  - Flexible terms
  - Potentially faster closing
  - No strict appraisal requirements
- **Considerations**:
  - Higher interest rates typically
  - Shorter term (often balloon payment)
  - Legal documentation critical

## Creative Financing Approaches

### 1. House Hacking Strategy
- **Approach**: Live in one unit while renting others to cover mortgage
- **Benefits**: 
  - Rental income offsets mortgage payment
  - Can qualify for owner-occupied financing
  - Lower down payment requirements
- **Considerations**:
  - Must comply with VA occupancy requirements
  - Property management responsibilities
  - Tenant selection critical

### 2. Partnership with Investor
- **Approach**: Partner with investor who provides down payment/closing costs
- **Structure Options**:
  - Equity sharing arrangement
  - Joint venture agreement
  - Silent partner arrangement
- **Benefits**:
  - Reduced capital needed upfront
  - Shared risk
- **Considerations**:
  - Clear legal agreement essential
  - Exit strategy should be defined
  - VA loan may have restrictions on co-borrowers

### 3. Lease-Option Strategy
- **Approach**: Lease with option to purchase
- **Benefits**:
  - Move in with minimal upfront costs
  - Time to improve credit/save for down payment
  - Lock in purchase price
- **Considerations**:
  - Higher monthly payments typically
  - Portion of rent may apply to purchase
  - Need strong legal agreement

### 4. Crowdfunding for Real Estate
- **Platforms**: Fundrise, RealtyMogul, etc.
- **Approach**: Raise capital from multiple small investors
- **Benefits**:
  - Access to capital without traditional lending
  - Potentially lower interest rates
  - Flexible terms
- **Considerations**:
  - Regulatory compliance important
  - Platform fees
  - Need compelling investment case

## Closing Cost Reduction Strategies

### 1. Negotiating Seller Concessions
- **Approach**: Ask seller to pay portion of closing costs
- **Typical Range**: Up to 4% of purchase price with VA loans
- **Strategy**: 
  - Offer slightly higher purchase price to offset concessions
  - Focus on specific high-cost items
  - Leverage property condition issues discovered in inspection

### 2. Lender Credits
- **Approach**: Accept slightly higher interest rate for credits toward closing costs
- **Benefit**: Reduced upfront cash needed
- **Consideration**: Higher monthly payment; more interest over loan term

### 3. Closing at Month-End
- **Strategy**: Close at end of month to reduce pre-paid interest
- **Savings**: Up to one month's interest
- **Coordination**: Work with lender and seller on timing

### 4. Title Company Shopping
- **Strategy**: Compare rates from multiple title companies
- **Potential Savings**: $500-1,000
- **Approach**: Ask lender for recommendations; compare online

### 5. Property Tax Prorations
- **Strategy**: Close after property taxes are paid
- **Benefit**: Reduced cash needed at closing
- **Consideration**: Timing constraints with seller

## Combining VA Loan with Other Programs

### Eligible Combinations
1. **VA Loan + Georgia Dream**: For down payment assistance
2. **VA Loan + Homes for Heroes**: For agent/lender rebates
3. **VA Loan + Seller Concessions**: For closing cost assistance
4. **VA Loan + Nonprofit Grants**: For down payment/closing costs

### Ineligible Combinations
1. **VA Loan + FHA/USDA Loans**: Cannot combine federal loan programs
2. **VA Loan + Certain Seller Financing Structures**: May violate VA requirements

### Process for Combining Programs
1. **Pre-Approval**: Get VA loan pre-approval first
2. **Secondary Program Application**: Apply for compatible assistance programs
3. **Coordination**: Ensure lender is experienced with combining programs
4. **Documentation**: Provide all program acceptance letters to all parties
5. **Closing Coordination**: Work with closing agent to properly document all funding sources

## Application Timeline and Process

### 1. Preparation Phase (1-2 months before property search)
- **VA Loan Pre-Approval**:
  - Submit Certificate of Eligibility request
  - Complete VA loan pre-approval application
  - Obtain pre-approval letter
  
- **Supplemental Program Applications**:
  - Research eligible programs based on location and status
  - Submit applications for down payment/closing cost assistance
  - Complete required homebuyer education courses

### 2. Property Search Phase
- **Property Requirements Verification**:
  - Ensure property meets VA requirements
  - Confirm property is in eligible area for supplemental programs
  - Preliminary assessment of property condition

### 3. Offer and Contract Phase
- **Offer Strategy**:
  - Include VA financing contingency
  - Request seller concessions for closing costs
  - Include inspection contingency
  
- **Contract Coordination**:
  - Ensure contract allows for all financing sources
  - Verify timeline aligns with all program requirements
  - Include appropriate contingencies

### 4. Loan Processing Phase (30-45 days)
- **VA Loan Processing**:
  - Submit complete application package
  - VA appraisal ordering and review
  - Address any property condition issues
  
- **Supplemental Funding Coordination**:
  - Submit award letters to VA lender
  - Coordinate funding timing with all sources
  - Complete any additional requirements

### 5. Closing Phase
- **Final Coordination**:
  - Confirm all funding sources are ready
  - Review Closing Disclosure for accuracy
  - Ensure all program requirements are met
  - Schedule closing to maximize benefits
