# GRID Alternatives Multifamily Program
## Application Information

### Program Overview
GRID Alternatives' Multifamily Program provides no-cost solar installations for affordable multifamily housing properties. The program aims to reduce energy costs for both property owners and residents, while providing environmental benefits and supporting workforce development through hands-on installation experience for job trainees.

### Eligibility
- **Property Types**:
  - Multifamily affordable housing (2+ units)
  - Both rental and resident-owned properties
  
- **Affordability Requirements**:
  - Properties must be deed-restricted affordable housing OR
  - At least 80% of residents must be at or below 80% of Area Median Income
  - Minimum 10 years remaining on affordability covenant (if applicable)
  
- **Technical Requirements**:
  - Suitable roof condition (less than 10 years old or recently replaced)
  - Minimal shading issues
  - Adequate electrical infrastructure
  - Sufficient space for solar equipment

### Program Benefits
- No-cost solar PV system installation
- Reduced operating costs for common areas
- Lower utility bills for residents (where applicable)
- Resident engagement and education
- Workforce development opportunities
- Environmental benefits and sustainability goals advancement

### Application Process
1. **Initial Contact**:
   - Complete interest form on GRID Alternatives website
   - Preliminary screening call with program staff
   
2. **Property Qualification**:
   - Submit property and affordability documentation
   - Initial technical feasibility assessment
   
3. **Site Assessment**:
   - On-site evaluation by GRID technical team
   - Roof assessment and electrical inspection
   - System sizing and design
   
4. **Proposal Development**:
   - System design and production estimates
   - Financial analysis and benefit allocation plan
   - Resident engagement strategy
   
5. **Funding Verification**:
   - Confirmation of program funding availability
   - Integration with other incentives if applicable
   
6. **Agreement Execution**:
   - Sign participation agreement
   - Finalize installation timeline
   
7. **Installation**:
   - Permitting and utility interconnection applications
   - Solar system installation
   - System commissioning and testing
   
8. **Post-Installation**:
   - Resident education workshops
   - System monitoring setup
   - Ongoing performance verification

### Required Documentation
- Property ownership documentation
- Proof of affordability restriction or resident income verification
- Utility bills for common areas and sample units
- Roof age certification or warranty information
- Site plans and electrical diagrams (if available)
- Property insurance documentation
- Entity formation documents (for ownership entity)

### Funding Structure
- **System Cost**: Fully covered by GRID Alternatives through:
  - State and federal incentives
  - Foundation grants
  - Corporate sponsorships
  - Individual donations
  
- **Owner Contribution**: None required, but optional contributions can:
  - Expand system size
  - Support additional workforce development
  - Fund resident engagement activities

### Application Timeline
- Initial screening: 1-2 weeks after inquiry
- Site assessment: 2-4 weeks after qualification
- Proposal development: 3-6 weeks after site assessment
- Funding verification: 4-8 weeks
- Installation: Typically within 6-12 months of agreement execution
- Total timeline: Approximately 12-18 months from initial inquiry to completion

### Special Considerations for Small Multifamily Properties (2-4 units)
- Streamlined application process
- Potential for expedited installation timeline
- May be eligible for both single-family and multifamily program models
- Consider whole-building approach for maximum benefits

### Resident Benefits
- Direct utility bill savings (for individually metered properties)
- Reduced common area costs potentially leading to stable rents
- Energy education and conservation training
- Potential job training opportunities during installation
- Improved property sustainability and quality

### Combining with Other Programs
- Can be integrated with weatherization assistance
- Compatible with utility energy efficiency programs
- May be coordinated with rehabilitation funding (CDBG, HOME)
- Can complement Low Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) properties

### Tips for Success
1. Address any deferred maintenance issues before application
2. Engage residents early in the process
3. Consider timing with roof replacement if needed
4. Prepare clear documentation of property affordability
5. Be prepared for a thorough technical assessment
6. Consider complementary energy efficiency improvements
