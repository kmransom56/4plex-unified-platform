# Good Capital Fund's Georgia Bright Program
## Application Information

### Program Overview
The Georgia Bright Program, operated by Good Capital Fund, offers low-cost solar leases specifically designed for Georgia residents, with a focus on making solar accessible to low-income communities. The program aims to reduce energy costs, increase clean energy adoption, and provide economic benefits to underserved communities.

### Eligibility
- **Geographic Requirements**:
  - Must be located in Georgia
  - Priority given to properties in low-income census tracts
  
- **Property Types**:
  - Single-family homes
  - Small multifamily properties (2-4 units)
  - Larger multifamily buildings (case-by-case basis)
  
- **Income Requirements**:
  - Program available to all income levels
  - Enhanced terms for households below 80% of Area Median Income (AMI)
  - Special financing for affordable housing providers
  
- **Property Requirements**:
  - Roof in good condition with 10+ years of remaining life
  - Sufficient sun exposure (minimal shading)
  - Electrical system compatible with solar installation

### Program Benefits
- No upfront costs for solar installation
- Low monthly lease payments (typically 10-30% below current electricity costs)
- System maintenance and monitoring included
- Option to purchase system at fair market value after 7 years
- Energy production guarantee
- Protection against utility rate increases

### Application Process
1. **Initial Inquiry**:
   - Complete online interest form
   - Provide basic property and contact information
   
2. **Preliminary Assessment**:
   - Remote roof and solar potential evaluation
   - Initial financial qualification review
   
3. **Site Assessment**:
   - On-site evaluation by solar professional
   - Detailed system design and production estimate
   
4. **Financial Review**:
   - Income verification (if applying for income-based terms)
   - Review of utility bills and energy usage
   - Credit check (minimum score requirements waived for income-qualified applicants)
   
5. **Lease Agreement**:
   - Review and sign solar lease agreement
   - Schedule installation
   
6. **Installation**:
   - System installation (typically 1-2 days)
   - Local permitting and inspections
   - Utility interconnection
   
7. **System Activation**:
   - Final inspection and commissioning
   - Customer orientation and education
   - Begin lease payments

### Required Documentation
- Property ownership documentation or landlord approval
- Recent utility bills (12 months preferred)
- Proof of income (for income-qualified terms)
- Photo ID
- Roof age documentation or certification
- For multifamily: tenant agreements or benefit sharing plan

### Lease Terms
- **Lease Length**: 20 years
- **Monthly Payment**: Based on system size and expected production
- **Escalator**: 0-2% annual increase (lower rates for income-qualified)
- **Maintenance**: Included for full lease term
- **Insurance**: Property owner's insurance required
- **Monitoring**: Real-time system monitoring included
- **Transferability**: Can transfer to new property owner

### Special Considerations for Multifamily Properties
- **Ownership Models**:
  - Common area meter connection
  - Individual unit connections
  - Virtual net metering (where available)
  
- **Tenant Benefits**:
  - Direct bill credits
  - Reduced common area charges
  - Stable or reduced utility costs
  
- **Additional Requirements**:
  - Benefit sharing agreement
  - Tenant notification plan
  - Property owner commitment to maintain affordability

### Application Timeline
- Initial assessment: 1-2 weeks after inquiry
- Site assessment scheduling: 1-3 weeks
- Lease approval: 2-4 weeks after site assessment
- Installation: 4-8 weeks after lease signing
- System activation: 2-4 weeks after installation (dependent on utility)

### Combining with Other Incentives
- Compatible with federal tax credits (property owner may assign benefits)
- Can be combined with Georgia Power rebates
- May be used alongside weatherization assistance
- Potential for integration with CDBG rehabilitation projects

### Tips for Success
1. Gather 12 months of utility bills before applying
2. Address any roof repairs before application
3. For multifamily properties, develop a clear tenant benefit plan
4. Consider combining with energy efficiency upgrades for maximum savings
5. Explore additional incentives that can be stacked with this program
