# VA Loan Closing Cost Assistance Programs
## Resources for Veterans Purchasing 2072 E Roxboro Rd NE

This guide focuses specifically on programs that can help cover closing costs when using your VA loan approval of up to $650,000 to purchase the multifamily property at 2072 E Roxboro Rd NE in Brookhaven.

## Understanding VA Loan Closing Costs

### Typical VA Loan Closing Costs
When purchasing with a VA loan, you can expect these closing costs:

1. **VA Funding Fee**: 1.25% - 3.3% of loan amount (varies based on down payment and first-time/subsequent use)
2. **Lender Origination Fee**: Typically 1% of loan amount
3. **Appraisal Fee**: $500-$800
4. **Title Services**: $1,000-$1,500
5. **Recording Fees**: $100-$200
6. **Credit Report**: $30-$50
7. **Survey**: $400-$600
8. **Inspection Fees**: $300-$500
9. **Prepaid Items**: 
   - Property taxes (varies)
   - Homeowners insurance (varies)
   - Interest (depends on closing date)

**Estimated Total**: $10,000-$20,000 for a $575,000 property (excluding funding fee which can be financed)

## VA-Specific Closing Cost Advantages

1. **Seller Concessions**: Sellers can contribute up to 4% of the purchase price toward closing costs
2. **Funding Fee Financing**: The VA funding fee can be included in the loan amount
3. **Non-Allowable Fees**: VA prohibits certain fees, reducing your costs
4. **Funding Fee Exemptions**: Veterans with service-connected disabilities may be exempt from the funding fee

## Closing Cost Assistance Programs for Veterans

### Federal Programs

#### 1. VA Adaptable Housing Grants
- **Overview**: For veterans with service-connected disabilities
- **Benefit**: Up to $101,754 for home adaptation
- **Application**: Through VA regional loan center
- **Website**: https://www.va.gov/housing-assistance/disability-housing-grants/

#### 2. HUD's Good Neighbor Next Door
- **Overview**: 50% discount on HUD homes for veterans and other eligible professionals
- **Benefit**: Substantial reduction in purchase price, reducing needed closing funds
- **Requirements**: Property must be in revitalization area; 36-month occupancy
- **Website**: https://www.hud.gov/program_offices/housing/sfh/reo/goodn/gnndabot

### Georgia State Programs

#### 1. Georgia Dream Down Payment Assistance
- **Overview**: Specifically can be used with VA loans
- **Benefit**: Up to $7,500 standard assistance ($10,000 for PEN program for veterans)
- **Can Cover**: Down payment and closing costs
- **Requirements**: 
  - Income limits (varies by county)
  - Purchase price limits
  - Homebuyer education course
- **Application**: Through participating lenders
- **Website**: https://www.dca.ga.gov/safe-affordable-housing/homeownership/georgia-dream

#### 2. Georgia Department of Veterans Service Benefits
- **Overview**: Various benefits for Georgia veterans
- **Benefit**: Potential closing cost grants and assistance
- **Application**: Through Georgia Department of Veterans Service
- **Website**: https://veterans.georgia.gov/

### DeKalb County Programs

#### 1. DeKalb County First-Time Homebuyer Program
- **Overview**: Can be combined with VA loans
- **Benefit**: Up to $10,000 in down payment and closing cost assistance
- **Format**: Forgivable loan (forgiven if you stay in home 5-10 years)
- **Requirements**: 
  - Income limits (80% of Area Median Income)
  - Property must be in DeKalb County
  - Homebuyer education required
- **Application**: Through DeKalb County Community Development
- **Website**: https://www.dekalbcountyga.gov/community-development/first-time-homebuyer-program

#### 2. Brookhaven Homeownership Program
- **Overview**: Local assistance for Brookhaven properties
- **Benefit**: Varies based on funding availability
- **Requirements**: Income limits apply
- **Contact**: Brookhaven Community Development Department
- **Phone**: 404-637-0500

### Nonprofit and Private Programs

#### 1. Dream Makers Program (PenFed Foundation)
- **Overview**: Specifically for veterans and military members
- **Benefit**: Grants up to $5,000 for down payment and closing costs
- **Requirements**: 
  - First-time homebuyer
  - Income requirements
  - Must contribute $500 of own funds
- **Application**: Through PenFed Foundation
- **Website**: https://penfedfoundation.org/apply-for-assistance/dream-makers/

#### 2. Homes for Heroes
- **Overview**: Rebates and discounts for veterans
- **Benefit**: Average savings of $2,400 through affiliated agents and lenders
- **How it Works**: Rebates on real estate commissions, lender fees, title fees
- **Application**: Register on their website
- **Website**: https://www.homesforheroes.com/

#### 3. NFCC Veteran Housing Assistance
- **Overview**: Financial coaching and grant assistance
- **Benefit**: Housing counseling and potential grant assistance
- **Application**: Through National Foundation for Credit Counseling
- **Website**: https://www.nfcc.org/

#### 4. USA Cares Housing Assistance
- **Overview**: Emergency housing assistance for veterans
- **Benefit**: Grants for housing costs including closing costs in some cases
- **Requirements**: Demonstrated need and military service
- **Website**: https://usacares.org/

## Strategies to Reduce or Cover Closing Costs

### 1. Seller Concessions
- **Strategy**: Negotiate for seller to pay closing costs
- **Maximum**: Up to 4% of purchase price with VA loans
- **Approach**: 
  - Offer slightly higher purchase price to offset concessions
  - Focus request on specific high-cost items
  - Use property inspection findings as leverage

### 2. Lender Credits
- **Strategy**: Accept slightly higher interest rate for lender credits
- **Benefit**: Can cover substantial portion of closing costs
- **Consideration**: Higher monthly payment; more interest over loan term
- **Approach**: Ask lender for various interest rate/credit scenarios

### 3. Real Estate Agent Commission Rebate
- **Strategy**: Work with an agent who offers commission rebates to veterans
- **Potential Savings**: 0.5%-1% of purchase price
- **Approach**: Interview agents about veteran rebate programs

### 4. Closing Cost Timing Strategies
- **Strategy**: Close at end of month to reduce pre-paid interest
- **Savings**: Up to one month's interest
- **Approach**: Coordinate with lender and seller on optimal closing date

### 5. Title Company Shopping
- **Strategy**: Compare rates from multiple title companies
- **Potential Savings**: $500-1,000
- **Approach**: Ask lender for recommendations; compare online

## Application Process for Closing Cost Assistance

### Step 1: VA Loan Pre-Approval
- Obtain Certificate of Eligibility (COE)
- Complete VA loan pre-approval application
- Determine closing cost needs

### Step 2: Program Research and Eligibility
- Review programs in this guide
- Determine eligibility for each program
- Prioritize programs based on benefits and requirements

### Step 3: Program Applications
- Complete applications for selected programs
- Gather required documentation
- Submit applications according to program timelines

### Step 4: Coordination with VA Lender
- Inform VA lender of all assistance programs
- Provide approval documentation
- Ensure lender experience with combining programs

### Step 5: Closing Coordination
- Confirm all assistance funds will be available at closing
- Review Closing Disclosure for accurate credits
- Coordinate timing of all funding sources

## Required Documentation Checklist

### For VA Loan
- Certificate of Eligibility (COE)
- DD-214 (military discharge papers)
- Recent pay stubs (30 days)
- W-2 forms and tax returns (2 years)
- Bank statements (2 months)
- Photo ID

### For Assistance Programs
- Proof of veteran status
- Income verification
- Property purchase contract
- Homebuyer education certificates
- First-time homebuyer affidavit (if applicable)
- Proof of funds for minimum contribution
- Budget worksheet
- Application forms for each program

## Action Plan for Securing Closing Cost Assistance

1. **Calculate Estimated Closing Costs**

2. **Apply for Georgia Dream Program**

3. **Register with Homes for Heroes**

   - Submit application package
   
2. **Contact DeKalb County First-Time Homebuyer Program**

### Tips for Success
1. **Apply Early**: Many programs have limited funding
2. **Apply to Multiple Programs**: Maximize potential assistance
3. **Coordinate Timing**: Ensure all programs align with purchase timeline
4. **Work with Experienced Professionals**: Find agents and lenders familiar with veteran programs
5. **Keep Detailed Records**: Maintain copies of all applications and communications
