# Creative Financing Options
## Innovative Strategies for Acquiring 2072 E Roxboro Rd NE

This document explores innovative and creative financing options beyond traditional VA loans and grant programs for acquiring the multifamily property at 2072 E Roxboro Rd NE in Brookhaven.

## Hybrid Financing Structures

### 1. VA Loan + Private Money Second Mortgage

**Structure:**
- Primary: VA loan for $575,000 (100% of purchase price)
- Secondary: Private money loan for renovation costs ($150,000-$175,000)

**How It Works:**
- Use VA loan for property acquisition with no down payment
- Close simultaneously with private second mortgage for renovations
- Private lender secures position with second lien on property

**Advantages:**
- No down payment required for purchase
- Avoids construction loan complexity
- Can close quickly

**Considerations:**
- Higher interest rate on second mortgage (typically 8-12%)
- May require personal guarantee
- Typically short-term (1-3 years) requiring refinance

**Potential Sources:**
- Private lenders specializing in renovation financing
- Family offices focused on real estate
- Local real estate investment groups
- Online platforms like PeerStreet or Fund That Flip

### 2. Subject-To Purchase with Seller Carryback for Renovations

**Structure:**
- Acquire property "subject to" existing mortgage (if any)
- Seller provides carryback financing for renovation costs

**How It Works:**
- Take over seller's existing mortgage payments
- Seller provides secondary financing for renovation budget
- Structure as interest-only with balloon payment

**Advantages:**
- Potentially lower closing costs
- No bank approval required
- Flexible terms negotiable with seller

**Considerations:**
- Requires seller with substantial equity
- Due-on-sale clause risk (though rarely enforced)
- Requires seller willing to remain financially involved

**Implementation Strategy:**
1. Research current mortgage on property
2. Present seller with benefits of continued interest income
3. Offer higher purchase price in exchange for favorable terms
4. Create strong legal documentation

### 3. Lease-Option with Renovation Clause

**Structure:**
- Long-term lease with option to purchase
- Renovation allowance built into agreement

**How It Works:**
- Secure 2-3 year lease with purchase option
- Negotiate renovation credit for improvements
- Apply portion of monthly rent toward purchase

**Advantages:**
- Minimal upfront capital required
- Time to improve property and increase value
- Opportunity to establish rental history

**Considerations:**
- Requires seller flexibility
- Need clear documentation of improvement value
- May limit some grant program eligibility

**Key Terms to Negotiate:**
- Option fee amount (typically 1-5% of purchase price)
- Monthly rent credit percentage (25-50% typical)
- Renovation approval process and value calculation
- Purchase price formula (fixed or calculation method)

## Partnership Structures

### 1. Equity Partnership with Service Component

**Structure:**
- Partner provides acquisition funding
- You provide property management and renovation oversight
- Equity split based on contributions

**How It Works:**
- Partner contributes cash for down payment/renovations
- You contribute VA loan eligibility and sweat equity
- Split cash flow and appreciation based on agreement

**Advantages:**
- Reduced capital requirement
- Leverages your VA eligibility and partner's cash
- Can structure to meet VA occupancy requirements

**Partnership Models:**
- **50/50 Split:** Equal sharing of costs and profits
- **Preferred Return:** Partner gets priority return on investment
- **Waterfall Structure:** Varying percentages based on performance
- **Buy-Out Provision:** Option to purchase partner's share over time

**Documentation Required:**
- Operating agreement with clearly defined roles
- Capital contribution schedule
- Distribution waterfall
- Exit strategy provisions
- Conflict resolution mechanism

### 2. Veteran-Focused Real Estate Investment Group

**Structure:**
- Form small investment group of fellow veterans
- Pool resources for down payment and renovations
- Use one member's VA eligibility for primary financing

**How It Works:**
- Create legal entity (LLC) with veteran members
- Each member contributes capital based on ownership percentage
- VA-eligible member occupies one unit
- Share in rental income from other units

**Advantages:**
- Shared risk and reduced individual capital requirement
- Leverages military network and shared experience
- May qualify for veteran business incentives

**Considerations:**
- Need clear operating agreement
- VA occupancy requirement falls on one member
- Exit strategy must be clearly defined

**Formation Steps:**
1. Identify 2-4 veteran partners with complementary skills
2. Create LLC with detailed operating agreement
3. Establish capital contribution requirements
4. Define management responsibilities
5. Create buy-sell provisions

### 3. Nonprofit Partnership for Affordable Housing

**Structure:**
- Partner with veteran-focused housing nonprofit
- Nonprofit provides partial funding through grants
- You provide VA loan eligibility and management

**How It Works:**
- Nonprofit secures grant funding for portion of acquisition/renovation
- You secure VA loan for remainder
- Agreement to maintain affordability for specified period

**Advantages:**
- Access to grant funding not available to individuals
- Potential property tax benefits
- Mission-aligned investment

**Potential Partners:**
- Veterans Community Project
- National Veterans Homeless Support
- Habitat for Humanity Veterans Build
- Local community development corporations

**Requirements:**
- Commitment to affordability standards
- Compliance with grant program requirements
- Regular reporting to nonprofit partner

## Creative Seller Financing Options

### 1. Installment Sale with Renovation Escrow

**Structure:**
- Seller finances entire purchase over 5-30 years
- Portion of price held in escrow for renovations

**How It Works:**
- Make regular payments directly to seller
- Draw from renovation escrow as work is completed
- Record land contract or mortgage for security

**Advantages:**
- No bank qualification required
- Potentially lower interest rate than hard money
- Flexible terms negotiable with seller

**Key Terms to Negotiate:**
- Interest rate (typically between bank rates and hard money)
- Term length (longer is better for you)
- Renovation draw schedule and approval process
- Prepayment options without penalty
- Deed transfer timing (immediate or upon milestone)

### 2. Seller Master Lease with Option

**Structure:**
- Master lease entire property with purchase option
- Sublease individual units to tenants
- Renovation rights included in master lease

**How It Works:**
- Pay seller fixed monthly amount
- Collect all rents and pay all expenses
- Apply portion of payment toward purchase option
- Complete renovations during lease period

**Advantages:**
- Control without ownership during renovation period
- Test rental market before committing to purchase
- Minimal closing costs

**Key Terms:**
- Lease term (3-5 years ideal)
- Option price calculation method
- Rent credit percentage
- Renovation approval process
- Default provisions

### 3. Seller Joint Venture

**Structure:**
- Form joint venture with property seller
- Seller contributes property, you contribute VA loan and management
- Share profits based on contribution value

**How It Works:**
- Create LLC with seller as member
- Seller contributes property at agreed valuation
- You secure financing and manage renovations/operations
- Split cash flow and eventual sale proceeds

**Advantages:**
- Seller remains invested in property success
- Reduces capital requirements
- Seller's knowledge of property and area

**Considerations:**
- Complex legal structure
- Need clear exit strategy
- Ongoing relationship with seller required

## Hybrid Public-Private Approaches

### 1. Opportunity Zone Fund Structure

**Structure:**
- Create or join Qualified Opportunity Zone Fund
- Fund provides equity for acquisition and renovation
- Defer capital gains taxes for fund investors

**How It Works:**
- Verify property is in Qualified Opportunity Zone
- Form legal entity meeting IRS requirements
- Raise capital from investors with capital gains
- Complete substantial improvements within 30 months

**Tax Benefits:**
- Investors defer capital gains taxes until 2026
- 10% reduction in deferred gain if held 5+ years
- No tax on new gains if investment held 10+ years

**Implementation Steps:**
1. Confirm Opportunity Zone status
2. Create fund structure with tax attorney
3. Develop investment prospectus
4. Target investors with recent capital gains

### 2. Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) Partnership

**Structure:**
- Partner with LIHTC developer or syndicator
- Allocate tax credits to investors in exchange for equity
- Commit to affordability requirements

**How It Works:**
- Apply for tax credit allocation through Georgia DCA
- Syndicate credits to investors (typically corporations)
- Receive equity investment equal to 70-90% of credits
- Maintain affordability for 15+ years

**Advantages:**
- Significant equity investment (potentially 50%+ of costs)
- Below-market financing options
- Property tax benefits in many jurisdictions

**Requirements:**
- Income restrictions on tenants (60% AMI typical)
- Rent restrictions based on AMI
- Compliance monitoring and reporting
- Long-term affordability commitment

### 3. Community Land Trust Model

**Structure:**
- Partner with local community land trust (CLT)
- CLT owns land, you own improvements
- Long-term ground lease (typically 99 years)

**How It Works:**
- CLT acquires land portion of property
- You purchase building improvements only
- Pay nominal ground lease fee to CLT
- Agree to affordability restrictions on resale

**Advantages:**
- Reduced acquisition cost (land value removed)
- Property tax savings in many jurisdictions
- Mission-aligned investment
- Potential grant funding access

**CLTs in Georgia:**
- Atlanta Land Trust
- Athens Land Trust
- Georgia Land Trust
