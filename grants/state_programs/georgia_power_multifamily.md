# Georgia Power Multifamily Home Energy Improvement Program
## Application Information

### Program Overview
The Multifamily Home Energy Improvement Program from Georgia Power offers rebates and incentives for energy efficiency improvements in multifamily properties. The program aims to help property owners reduce energy consumption, lower utility bills, and improve comfort for residents.

### Eligibility
- **Property Types**:
  - Multifamily buildings (2+ units)
  - Apartments
  - Condominiums
  - Townhomes
  - Student housing
  
- **Service Requirements**:
  - Must be a Georgia Power customer
  - Property must be in Georgia Power service territory
  
- **Building Requirements**:
  - Existing buildings (new construction not eligible)
  - Both market-rate and affordable housing qualify

### Program Options
1. **Whole Building Approach**:
   - Comprehensive energy assessment
   - Custom package of improvements
   - Higher rebate potential
   
2. **Individual Improvement Approach**:
   - Select specific measures from prescriptive list
   - No comprehensive assessment required
   - Simpler process but potentially lower rebates

### Application Process
1. **Initial Contact**:
   - Complete interest form on Georgia Power website
   - Schedule initial consultation with program representative
   
2. **Program Enrollment**:
   - Sign program participation agreement
   - Select a participating contractor or use your own qualified contractor
   
3. **Energy Assessment**:
   - $75 for a professional energy assessment
   - Identifies energy-saving opportunities
   - Establishes baseline energy usage
   
4. **Project Proposal**:
   - Contractor develops proposal with eligible improvements
   - Estimated energy savings and rebate amounts calculated
   
5. **Pre-Approval**:
   - Submit project for pre-approval
   - Receive confirmation of eligible rebate amounts
   
6. **Installation**:
   - Complete energy efficiency improvements
   - Contractor performs work according to program standards
   
7. **Test-Out Assessment**:
   - Post-installation assessment verifies proper installation
   - Confirms energy savings potential
   
8. **Rebate Processing**:
   - Submit completion documentation
   - Receive rebate check (typically within 4-6 weeks)

### Eligible Improvements & Rebate Amounts
- **HVAC**:
  - Central AC replacement: $50 per ton
  - Heat pump replacement: $100 per ton
  - Smart thermostats: $75 each
  
- **Building Envelope**:
  - Attic insulation: $0.15 per square foot
  - Air sealing: $0.35 per CFM50 reduction
  - Duct sealing: $3 per ton
  
- **Water Heating**:
  - Heat pump water heater: $250 per unit
  - Low-flow showerheads: $20 each
  - Faucet aerators: $5 each
  
- **Lighting**:
  - LED fixtures: $15-30 per fixture
  - Occupancy sensors: $15 each
  
- **Appliances**:
  - ENERGY STAR refrigerator: $50
  - ENERGY STAR clothes washer: $50

### Required Documentation
- Georgia Power account information
- Property ownership documentation
- W-9 form for rebate payment
- Contractor proposals and estimates
- Equipment specifications
- Paid invoices for all completed work
- Before and after photos
- Energy assessment results

### Application Timeline
- Applications accepted year-round
- Pre-approval typically takes 5-10 business days
- Projects must be completed within 90 days of pre-approval
- Rebate processing takes 4-6 weeks after final documentation submission

### Special Considerations for Small Multifamily Properties (2-4 units)
- May qualify for both residential and multifamily programs
- Individual unit improvements can be combined with whole-building approaches
- Common area improvements eligible if they benefit residential units
- Consider coordinating with tenant utility accounts for maximum benefits

### Tips for Success
1. Start with the energy assessment to identify the most cost-effective improvements
2. Bundle multiple improvements to maximize rebates and energy savings
3. Consider improvements that address both common areas and individual units
4. Coordinate with planned maintenance or renovation projects
5. Educate tenants about energy-saving behaviors to maximize benefits
6. Consider combining with other incentives like Georgia's Home Energy Rebates
7. Keep detailed records of all communications and documentation
