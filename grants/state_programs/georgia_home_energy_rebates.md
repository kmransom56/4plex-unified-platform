# Georgia's Home Energy Rebates
## Application Information

### Program Overview
Georgia's Home Energy Rebates program provides incentives for energy-efficient home improvements, including appliances, insulation, air sealing, HVAC systems, and other energy-saving measures. The program is funded through the Inflation Reduction Act and administered by the Georgia Environmental Finance Authority (GEFA).

### Program Components
1. **Home Electrification and Appliance Rebates (HEAR)**: Income-qualified rebates for electric appliances and other upgrades
2. **Home Efficiency Rebates (HER)**: Performance-based rebates for whole-home energy savings

### Eligibility
- **Property Types**:
  - Single-family homes
  - Small multifamily buildings (2-4 units)
  - Manufactured homes
  - Condominiums and townhomes
  
- **Income Requirements**:
  - HEAR Program: Household income at or below 150% of Area Median Income (AMI)
  - Enhanced HEAR rebates: Household income at or below 80% of AMI
  - HER Program: Available to all income levels, with enhanced rebates for low and moderate income households
  
- **Property Requirements**:
  - Must be located in Georgia
  - Must be an existing residential building (new construction not eligible)
  - Must be used as primary residence for at least half the year

### Rebate Amounts
#### HEAR Program (Income-Qualified)
- Heat pump water heater: up to $1,750
- Heat pump HVAC system: up to $8,000
- Electric stove/cooktop: up to $840
- Heat pump clothes dryer: up to $840
- Electrical panel upgrade: up to $4,000
- Insulation, air sealing, ventilation: up to $1,600
- Electric wiring: up to $2,500

#### HER Program (Performance-Based)
- Energy savings of 20-34%: up to $2,000
- Energy savings of 35% or more: up to $4,000
- For low-income households (below 80% AMI):
  - Energy savings of 20-34%: up to $4,000
  - Energy savings of 35% or more: up to $8,000

### Application Process
1. **Find a Participating Contractor**:
   - Visit the Georgia Energy Rebates website
   - Search for contractors in your area who are registered with the program
   
2. **Home Energy Assessment**:
   - Contractor performs pre-upgrade assessment
   - Identifies eligible improvements
   - Estimates potential energy savings
   
3. **Rebate Reservation**:
   - Contractor submits rebate reservation request
   - Receives approval before beginning work
   
4. **Installation**:
   - Contractor completes eligible improvements
   - For HER program, conducts post-upgrade assessment to verify energy savings
   
5. **Rebate Processing**:
   - Contractor submits completion documentation
   - Rebate is processed (point-of-sale or reimbursement)
   - Homeowner pays reduced cost or receives reimbursement

### Required Documentation
- Proof of property ownership or landlord permission
- Proof of income (for income-qualified rebates)
- Utility bills for the past 12 months
- Property tax records
- Contractor certification and licensing information
- Pre and post-installation photos
- Product specifications for installed equipment
- Paid invoices for all work completed

### Application Timeline
- Program launched in early 2024
- Rebates available until funding is depleted (first-come, first-served)
- Processing time: 2-4 weeks after submission of complete documentation
- Reimbursement typically received within 4-6 weeks of approval

### Special Considerations for Multifamily Properties
- For 2-4 unit buildings, each unit may qualify separately
- Common area improvements may be eligible if they directly benefit residential units
- For rental properties:
  - Landlord must provide tenant authorization
  - Benefits must flow to tenants (e.g., reduced utility bills)
  - Rent increases may be restricted for a certain period

### Tips for Success
1. Work only with contractors registered with the program
2. Get multiple quotes to ensure competitive pricing
3. Consider bundling multiple improvements to maximize rebates
4. For multifamily properties, coordinate with tenants early in the process
5. Keep detailed records of all communications and documentation
6. Consider combining with other incentives like utility rebates and tax credits
