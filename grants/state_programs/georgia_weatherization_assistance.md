# Georgia Weatherization Assistance Program
## Application Information

### Program Overview
The Georgia Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP) increases the energy efficiency of dwellings owned or occupied by low-income persons, reducing their energy costs while ensuring their health and safety. The program is administered by the Georgia Environmental Finance Authority (GEFA) and implemented through local Community Action Agencies.

### Eligibility
- **Income Requirements**: Households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level
- **Priority Given To**:
  - Elderly (60 years or older)
  - Persons with disabilities
  - Families with children under 18
  - Households with high energy burden
- **Property Types**:
  - Single-family homes
  - Small multifamily buildings (2-4 units)
  - Larger multifamily buildings (with additional requirements)
- **Ownership Status**:
  - Both owner-occupied and rental properties are eligible
  - For rental properties, landlord agreement is required

### Application Process
1. **Identify Local Provider**:
   - Contact GEFA to find your local Community Action Agency or service provider
   - Each county in Georgia is served by a specific provider

2. **Initial Application**:
   - Complete the application form from your local provider
   - Provide required documentation
   - Submit to local Community Action Agency

3. **Energy Audit**:
   - If application is approved, an energy auditor will conduct an assessment
   - Audit identifies energy efficiency measures with the highest return

4. **Work Scope Development**:
   - Based on audit results, a work scope is developed
   - Prioritizes health and safety concerns and cost-effective measures

5. **Contractor Selection**:
   - Local agency selects qualified contractors
   - Work is scheduled with property owner's approval

6. **Installation and Inspection**:
   - Energy efficiency measures are installed
   - Final inspection verifies quality and completion

### Required Documentation
- Proof of income for all household members
- Property ownership documentation or landlord permission
- Utility bills for the past 12 months
- Proof of identity (driver's license, state ID, etc.)
- Social Security cards for all household members
- Proof of residence in Georgia

### Funding Amount
- Average of $6,500-$8,000 per dwelling unit
- No direct cost to eligible participants
- For multifamily buildings, funding may be available per unit

### Application Timeline
- Applications accepted year-round
- Processing time varies by local agency (typically 4-12 weeks)
- Work typically completed within 3-6 months of application approval
- Waitlists may exist in some areas

### Eligible Weatherization Measures
- Air sealing (caulking, weather-stripping)
- Attic, wall, and floor insulation
- HVAC system repairs or replacement
- Water heater repair or replacement
- Energy-efficient lighting
- Refrigerator replacement (if qualified)
- Window and door repairs or replacements
- Ventilation improvements
- Health and safety measures

### Special Considerations for Multifamily Properties
- All units in 2-4 unit buildings must be income-qualified
- For buildings with 5+ units, at least 66% of units must be income-qualified
- Landlord agreement required, including:
  - Maintaining affordable rent for at least 12 months
  - Not increasing rent due to weatherization improvements
  - Giving preference to low-income tenants for 12 months

### Tips for Success
1. Apply early as waitlists can be long in some areas
2. Gather all required documentation before applying
3. Be prepared to provide access to all areas of the property
4. Respond promptly to any requests for additional information
5. For multifamily properties, work with all tenants to ensure cooperation
