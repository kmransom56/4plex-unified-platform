# Georgia HOME Investment Partnership Program
## Application Information

### Program Overview
The HOME Investment Partnership Program (HOME) is a federally funded program administered by the Georgia Department of Community Affairs (DCA). It provides funding for a wide range of activities including building, buying, and/or rehabilitating affordable housing for rent or homeownership, or providing direct rental assistance to low-income people.

### Eligibility
- **Eligible Applicants**:
  - Local governments (participating jurisdictions)
  - Community Housing Development Organizations (CHDOs)
  - For-profit developers
  - Nonprofit organizations
  
- **Property Types**:
  - Single-family homes
  - Small multifamily properties (2-4 units)
  - Larger multifamily developments
  
- **Income Requirements**:
  - Rental housing: 90% of units must be occupied by households at or below 60% AMI
  - All units must be occupied by households at or below 80% AMI
  - For rental projects with 5+ HOME-assisted units, 20% must be occupied by households at or below 50% AMI

### Eligible Activities
- Acquisition of property
- Rehabilitation of existing structures
- New construction
- Reconstruction
- Site improvements
- Demolition
- Energy efficiency improvements
- Utility connections
- Tenant-based rental assistance

### Funding Amounts
- **Maximum Per-Unit Subsidy**: Varies by bedroom size and location
  - 0 Bedroom: $153,314
  - 1 Bedroom: $175,752
  - 2 Bedroom: $213,718
  - 3 Bedroom: $276,482
  - 4+ Bedroom: $303,490
  
- **Typical Award Ranges**:
  - Small multifamily rehabilitation: $50,000-$200,000
  - New construction: $500,000-$2,000,000
  - Maximum award depends on project size and available funding

### Application Process
1. **Notice of Funding Availability (NOFA)**:
   - DCA issues NOFA announcing available funding
   - Includes application requirements and deadlines
   
2. **Pre-Application**:
   - Submit letter of intent
   - Preliminary project description
   - Developer qualifications
   
3. **Application Submission**:
   - Complete full application package
   - Submit all required documentation
   - Pay application fee
   
4. **Application Review**:
   - Threshold review for completeness
   - Scoring based on established criteria
   - Site visits for competitive applications
   
5. **Award Announcement**:
   - Notification of preliminary award
   - Underwriting and due diligence
   
6. **Commitment**:
   - Execute HOME written agreement
   - Meet all pre-construction requirements
   
7. **Implementation**:
   - Complete project according to agreement
   - Submit regular progress reports
   - Comply with all program requirements

### Required Documentation
- Application form
- Project description and timeline
- Development budget and pro forma
- Sources and uses statement
- Evidence of site control
- Zoning compliance verification
- Market study or analysis
- Environmental review documentation
- Architectural plans and specifications
- Developer/owner capacity documentation
- Match commitment documentation
- Affirmative marketing plan
- Tenant selection policies
- Energy efficiency plans

### Application Timeline
- Applications typically accepted once per year
- Annual NOFA released in first quarter
- Application deadline: 60 days after NOFA
- Review period: 90-120 days
- Award announcements: 30-60 days after review
- Project completion deadline: 24 months from commitment

### Compliance Requirements
- **Affordability Period**:
  - Rehabilitation (under $15,000 per unit): 5 years
  - Rehabilitation ($15,000-$40,000 per unit): 10 years
  - Rehabilitation (over $40,000 per unit): 15 years
  - New construction or acquisition of new housing: 20 years
  
- **Other Requirements**:
  - Davis-Bacon wage requirements (12+ units)
  - Environmental review
  - Lead-based paint regulations
  - Fair housing and accessibility standards
  - Energy efficiency standards
  - Regular reporting and monitoring

### Special Considerations for Small Multifamily Properties
- May be eligible for streamlined processing
- Can be combined with other funding sources like CDBG
- Energy efficiency improvements are eligible costs
- Consider applying through a local participating jurisdiction for potentially faster processing

### Tips for Success
1. Attend DCA HOME program workshops and training sessions
2. Consult with DCA staff early in the planning process
3. Ensure project meets all threshold requirements before applying
4. Develop a strong match contribution plan
5. Incorporate energy efficiency measures to improve scoring
6. Consider partnering with experienced developers or CHDOs
7. Prepare a detailed and realistic development schedule
8. Have site control secured before application
