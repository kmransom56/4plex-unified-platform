# DeKalb County CDBG Program
## Comprehensive Application Guide

### Program Overview
The Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) program in DeKalb County provides funding for housing rehabilitation, public facilities, infrastructure improvements, and public services. For small multifamily property owners, the Housing Rehabilitation component offers significant opportunities for property improvements, including energy efficiency upgrades.

### Eligibility Requirements
- **Property Location**: Must be located within DeKalb County (including Brookhaven)
- **Property Type**: Small multifamily properties (2-4 units)
- **Ownership**: Must own the property for at least one year prior to application
- **Occupancy**: At least 51% of units must be occupied by low-to-moderate income households (below 80% AMI)
- **Property Condition**: Must need rehabilitation to meet housing quality standards
- **Property Value**: After-rehabilitation value cannot exceed 95% of the median home value in DeKalb County
- **Taxes and Insurance**: Must be current on property taxes and have adequate insurance
- **Financial Status**: Must be able to maintain the property after rehabilitation

### Eligible Rehabilitation Activities
- **Health and Safety Repairs**:
  - Roof repair/replacement
  - Electrical system upgrades
  - Plumbing repairs
  - Lead-based paint remediation
  - Asbestos removal
  
- **Energy Efficiency Improvements**:
  - Insulation installation
  - Window and door replacement
  - HVAC system upgrades
  - Energy-efficient appliances
  - Water conservation measures
  
- **Accessibility Modifications**:
  - Ramps and lifts
  - Bathroom modifications
  - Doorway widening
  - Accessible fixtures
  
- **General Rehabilitation**:
  - Structural repairs
  - Siding and exterior improvements
  - Interior renovations
  - Site improvements

### Funding Amounts
- **Maximum Award**: Up to $50,000 per unit
- **Form of Assistance**: Forgivable loan (typically forgiven over 5-15 years)
- **Owner Contribution**: Minimum 10% match may be required for some components
- **Affordability Period**: 5-15 years depending on assistance amount

### Application Process
1. **Pre-Application**:
   - Attend information session (offered quarterly)
   - Submit pre-application form
   - Initial eligibility screening
   
2. **Full Application**:
   - Complete detailed application form
   - Submit all required documentation
   - Property inspection scheduling
   
3. **Property Assessment**:
   - Initial property inspection
   - Lead and asbestos testing if needed
   - Development of work specifications
   
4. **Underwriting and Approval**:
   - Financial review
   - Project feasibility assessment
   - Loan committee review
   - Award notification
   
5. **Pre-Construction**:
   - Contractor selection (from approved list)
   - Contract execution
   - Loan closing
   
6. **Construction**:
   - Notice to proceed
   - Construction monitoring
   - Progress inspections
   - Change order approval (if needed)
   
7. **Completion**:
   - Final inspection
   - Contractor payment
   - Project closeout
   - Begin compliance period

### Required Documentation
- **Property Ownership**:
  - Deed or title
  - Property tax records
  - Mortgage statement
  
- **Financial Information**:
  - Bank statements (3 months)
  - Tax returns (2 years)
  - Income verification
  - Debt schedule
  
- **Property Information**:
  - Property insurance
  - Flood insurance (if in flood zone)
  - Utility bills
  - Existing tenant information
  
- **Project Planning**:
  - Scope of work (if already developed)
  - Cost estimates (if available)
  - Energy audit results (if completed)
  - Photos of areas needing repair
  
- **Tenant Information**:
  - Current rent roll
  - Tenant income certifications
  - Leases for all units

### Application Timeline
- **Application Cycle**: Quarterly (January, April, July, October)
- **Review Period**: 60-90 days from submission
- **Award Notification**: Within 30 days of review completion
- **Project Completion**: Must be completed within 12 months of award

### Compliance Requirements
- **Tenant Income Verification**:
  - Annual recertification during affordability period
  - Minimum 51% LMI occupancy maintained
  
- **Rent Restrictions**:
  - Rents must remain affordable to LMI households
  - Annual rent increases limited during affordability period
  
- **Property Standards**:
  - Maintain property in good condition
  - Annual inspections may be conducted
  
- **Reporting Requirements**:
  - Annual compliance reports
  - Tenant demographic information
  - Property financial information

### Special Considerations for Energy Efficiency
- **Enhanced Funding**: Additional funding may be available for energy efficiency components
- **Technical Assistance**: Free energy audits may be provided
- **Utility Coordination**: Assistance with utility rebate applications
- **Green Building**: Incentives for meeting green building standards
- **Solar Integration**: Support for integrating solar with rehabilitation

### Combining with Other Programs
The CDBG program can be effectively combined with:
- Georgia Weatherization Assistance Program
- Georgia Power rebates and incentives
- Federal tax credits for energy efficiency
- Solar incentives and financing programs
- HOME Investment Partnership funds (for larger projects)

### Application Workshops
DeKalb County offers quarterly application workshops to assist property owners with the CDBG application process. Workshop dates are posted on the county website and pre-registration is required.

### Tips for a Successful Application
1. **Start Early**: Begin gathering documentation well before the application deadline
2. **Attend a Workshop**: County staff can provide valuable guidance
3. **Conduct an Energy Audit**: Demonstrates commitment to energy efficiency
4. **Develop a Clear Scope**: Prioritize health, safety, and energy efficiency
5. **Document Tenant Income**: Ensure proper income verification for all tenants
6. **Prepare a Realistic Budget**: Include contingencies for unexpected issues
7. **Consider Phasing**: For larger projects, consider a phased approach
8. **Highlight Benefits**: Clearly articulate benefits to low-income residents
9. **Demonstrate Capacity**: Show ability to manage the project and maintain the property
10. **Follow Up**: Maintain communication with program staff throughout the process

### Common Reasons for Denial
- Incomplete application
- Insufficient documentation
- Property not meeting eligibility criteria
- Owner financial capacity concerns
- Project scope beyond program parameters
- Insufficient tenant income documentation
- Property value exceeding limits
- Past compliance issues

### Appeals Process
If your application is denied, you may appeal the decision within 30 days by submitting a written request for reconsideration to the Community Development Department Director.
